Chart 1: Global Primary Demand vs Primary Supply (2025-2035)

Primary demand grows from 96.6 kt/yr (2025) to 120 kt/yr (2035). Primary supply peaks at 96.5 kt/yr (2032) then declines. Deficit widens to -25 kt/yr by 2035.
Key insight: Global primary supply peaks in 2032-2033 at 96-97 kt/yr, then declines as China's depletion accelerates faster than ex-China new mines ramp. The deficit grows from -11.5 kt/yr (2025) to -25 kt/yr (2035) — a 118% widening.

Chart 2: China Primary Supply vs Ex-China Primary Supply (2025-2035)

China declines from 67 → 49 kt/yr (-27%). Ex-China grows from 18 → 46 kt/yr (+154%) driven by TGN, Almonty, Mactung, Cove, EQR.
Key insight: China's share of global primary supply falls from 79% (2025) to 52% (2035). The ex-China ramp is necessary because China's decline is structural (declining grades 0.42% → 0.28%, aging mines, environmental enforcement, +100,000 yuan/t mining cost).

Chart 3: Ex-China Primary Demand vs Supply (2025-2035)

Ex-China is structurally short of primary supply. Deficit peaks at -16.9 kt/yr (2025), narrows to ~0 kt/yr by 2032, then opens again as ex-China supply plateaus.
Critical: Until 2032, ex-China primary supply cannot meet ex-China primary demand. The gap is filled by Chinese imports (concentrate, APT, AMT) — but Chinese export controls (Feb 2025) make this unreliable. The structural ex-China deficit is the binding constraint for DFARS-compliant buyers.

Chart 4: Ex-China Structural Deficit WITHOUT TGN/Cove/Mactung

The bear scenario: if the new wave of mines (TGN, Cove, Mactung) fails to ramp on schedule, ex-China remains in deficit of 8-17 kt/yr through 2030 and 1.5-2.5 kt/yr through 2035.
TGN's marginal value: Without TGN, ex-China would be in deficit of 13,160 t/yr in 2027 and 8,003 t/yr in 2030. TGN's Watershed alone (1,657 t W) covers 12.6% of the 2027 gap. TGN's full ramp (8,861 t W) covers 110% of the 2030 gap.

Chart 5: TGN Production Ramp by Asset (Watershed + Mt Mulgine + Hatches Creek)

TGN ramps from 0 (2025) → 1,657 t W (2027 Watershed) → 8,861 t W (2030 Mt Mulgine 6 Mtpa) → 14,261 t W (2032 Mt Mulgine 15 Mtpa) → 15,054 t W (2035 full multi-asset).
TGN's growth trajectory: From 2027 to 2035 (8 years), TGN's production grows 9.1x (1,657 → 15,054 t/yr W). This is the steepest ramp of any ex-China Tier 2 producer.

Chart 6: TGN's Share of Ex-China Primary Supply (2027-2035)

TGN's share of ex-China primary supply grows from 6% (2027) to 32% (2035). TGN becomes the single largest ex-China primary producer.
Market dominance: By 2035, TGN alone accounts for 32% of ex-China primary supply. Add Almonty Sangdong (10%), Mactung (8%), and the top 4 producers control 60% of ex-China primary supply. The market is concentrating rapidly.

Chart 7: TGN's Coverage of Ex-China Structural Deficit (2027-2035)

TGN's production vs the ex-China structural deficit (assuming TGN, Cove, Mactung all ramp). TGN covers 12.6% of 2027 gap → 110% of 2030 gap → 600%+ of 2035 gap.
Single most consequential supplier: TGN's full multi-asset production (15,054 t/yr W) exceeds the entire ex-China structural deficit (2,503 t/yr) by 6x by 2035. TGN is the necessary supply addition, not optional.

Chart 8: If-China-Weaponizes Stress Test (Ex-China Self-Sufficiency Gap)

If China escalates to full Stage 1-5 squeeze (cut all tungsten exports), ex-China self-sufficiency gap becomes 45-55 kt/yr in 2025, 38-48 kt/yr in 2027, 25-32 kt/yr in 2030, 15-22 kt/yr in 2035.
Weaponized scenario: If China cuts ALL tungsten exports (concentrate, APT, AMT, 6N/7N, WF6), ex-China self-sufficiency gap is 45-55 kt/yr in 2025. Even with full TGN + Almonty + Mactung + Cove ramp, the 2035 gap is still 15-22 kt/yr. APT price would spike to $3,000-4,300/MTU; TGN re-rates to A$8-15.

Chart 9: Ex-China Mine-by-Mine Production Ramp (Stacked)

Major ex-China mines by start year: Almonty Sangdong (2026), Gentung (H2 2026), TGN Watershed (Q2 2027), EQR Barruecopardo (2027), Masan Expansion (2028), TGN Mt Mulgine (Q3 2029), Mactung (2030), Cove (2030), TGN 15 Mtpa (2032).
2027-2030 is the critical ramp window: Six major mines (Almonty P1+P2, Gentung, TGN Watershed, EQR Barruecopardo, Masan Expansion) come online in this window, adding ~14,000 t/yr W. From 2030-2033, Mactung, Cove, TGN Mt Mulgine 15 Mtpa add another 18,000+ t/yr W.
