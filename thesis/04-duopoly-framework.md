**TAA vs RDP Legal Precision:**

The market conflates two distinct legal frameworks. They are NOT the same:
- **TAA (Trade Agreements Act, 19 U.S.C. § 2511):** Commercial/FTA procurement. Waives Buy American Act primarily for **end products**. Raw materials face "substantial transformation" test and DCAA audit friction.
- **RDP MOU (Reciprocal Defense Procurement):** Pre-cleared bilateral defense treaty. **Raw minerals automatically eligible** for DoD procurement; no per-contract paperwork.

**The 28 DFARS 225.872-1 Qualifying Countries (RDP) are NOT identical to the TAA Designated Countries.** South Korea is on the TAA list (KORUS FTA 2012, WTO GPA) but NOT on the DFARS qualifying list. This is the core legal precision that demotes Almonty from "Tier 2 frictionless partner" to "Tier 3 operational incumbent with per-contract friction."

**TGN's 12-18 month US DoD captive window (Q4 2026 - Q4 2027):** Only period when TGN has both (a) RDP-qualifying frictionless status AND (b) bulk-tonnage open-pit production at scale + (c) no US Tier 1 competition. After Q4 2027, US Tier 1 mines (TUNGF Phase II, Blue Moon Springer, Guardian Pilot Mountain) close the US DoD window. Mactung (also RDP-qualifying) shares Tier 2 from 2030-2031 onwards.

**Almonty's TPW offtake (May 7, 2025) is pre-DFARS:** It was a commercial transaction before DFARS 252.225-7052 took effect. It validates Almonty's commercial viability and customer relationship, but does NOT validate Almonty's post-Jan 1, 2027 DoD access without per-contract nonavailability exception.

**RDP-A Negotiations Status (— confirmed stalled):** The U.S.-South Korea Reciprocal Defense Procurement Agreement (RDP-A) was announced as "in negotiation" in the Feb 23, 2024 Federal Register. As of November 2025, negotiations remain **stalled**:

- **Korea JoongAng Daily (Sep 29, 2025):** "Negotiations with Brazil, India and Korea have stalled, as all require foreign contractors to provide investment, technology transfer or industrial cooperation"
- **ICKS paper:** "Despite over 30 years of U.S.-ROK dialogue, significant differences remained unsolved"
- Central obstacle: U.S. wants "full reciprocal access" without Korean mandatory offset programs; Korea insists offsets are essential to industrial development

**Implication:** No imminent signing date for U.S.-South Korea RDP-A. South Korea will likely remain Tier 3 (TAA-only) for the foreseeable future, sustaining TGN's frictionless defense pipeline advantage.

## 5-Layer Supply Chain Chokepoints

> **CHOKEPOINTS note:** Earlier drafts of this thesis used the phrase "supply chain chokepoint" as if the tungsten chain had a single chokepoint. It does not. The chain has **five distinct layers, each with its own ex-China capacity, growth trajectory, and price premium**. The historical Tier 2/3 duopoly framework in this file is **jurisdictional** (who can legally supply the U.S. defense market); the 5-layer framework below is **processing-stage** (where refining capacity is concentrated). They are independent and must not be conflated.
>
> **Three common errors this audit corrects:**
> 1. **"APT is THE chokepoint"** — APT is A chokepoint, specifically the **gateway** (75-80% of all W passes through APT). It is not the only chokepoint. The most binding constraint is the **6N/7N layer** (Layer 4), which is 0% ex-China today and projected to remain 60-80% in deficit through 2030.
> 2. **"Mining is the chokepoint"** — Mining (Layer 1) is the **softest** layer. Ex-China mines (Almonty, Masan, Plansee, TGN, Mactung, EQR, KIS) collectively produce ~20% of global W and bid into a competitive APT refiner market. TGN's Watershed concentrate is in a buyer's market.
> 3. **"DFARS defense chain" conflated with "semi WF6 chain"** — The 5 layers serve **two independent end-markets**: (a) DFARS defense (carbide, armor-piercing, electronics) is gated by Tier 2 jurisdictional access AND Layer 1+2 processing; (b) Semi WF6 is gated by Layer 4 (6N/7N) and Layer 5 (WF6 conversion). The Jan 1, 2027 DFARS cliff does not solve the semi WF6 gap; that gap is closed by H.C. Starck 50% expansion, CSSC +1,000 t/yr, JNM doubling, Sumitomo 50% new plant.
