### The DFARS Cliff Is NOT a Supply Chain Catalyst

The Jan 1, 2027 DFARS cliff is a **regulatory** catalyst, not a supply-chain capacity catalyst. It restricts which producers can SELL to the U.S. defense market; it does not add new mining/refining capacity. The supply-side catalysts are:

- **Layer 1 (mine):** TGN Watershed 2027, TGN Mt Mulgine 2029, Mactung 2030-31
- **Layer 3 (oxide):** H.C. Starck 50% expansion 2027
- **Layer 4 (6N/7N):** JNM doubling, Sumitomo 50% new plant (2027-2030 buildout timeline, industry-wide Western 6N/7N capacity, not the US DoD captive window)
- **Layer 5 (WF6):** CSSC +1,000 t/yr Q1 2027; Kanto/Central Glass status fluid

**TGN's catalysts (FID, first production) address Layer 1, the SOFTEST layer.** The binding constraints (6N/7N, WF6) are addressed by OTHER companies (H.C. Starck, JNM, Sumitomo, CSSC) and are NOT under TGN's control. This means TGN benefits INDIRECTLY when those companies add capacity (more bidders for ex-China concentrate) but does not capture the 6N/WF6 premiums directly.
