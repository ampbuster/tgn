## 8. Recycling Scales Up Dramatically

**Trigger:** Sandvik/Kennametal's 100% recycled carbide tools + EU CRMA recycling mandates scale up

> **v10.1 REFRAMING (AUDIT-2, July 18, 2026):** The earlier "Recycling Scales Up Dramatically" failure mode used a single global recycling rate / blended ceiling framing. The correct framing is **industry-specific** — recycling rates differ vastly by end-use. The 30-40% claim was an over-estimate; the real blended ceiling is ~30% (already realized) and the marginal recycling growth is concentrated in **cemented carbide (already near ceiling) + WHA (mature)**, NOT in the 28-44 kt/yr "permanent primary demand floor" where recycling CANNOT close the gap (chemistry-bound chemicals + pyrometallurgical losses in steel/superalloy).

**Mechanism (corrected by industry):**
- Sandvik: 100% recycled carbide tools (Nov 2023) — **cemented carbide segment only, already at 90% rate (ceiling)**
- Kennametal: closed-loop recycling (Aug 2023) — **cemented carbide segment only, already at 88% rate (ceiling)**
- EU CRMA mandates 25% recycled content by 2030 — **applies to all W products, but the chemistry-bound segments (WF6, SCR, AMT, WS2, pigments) cannot reach 25%**
- **If blended recycling hits 35-40% (realistic ceiling, NOT 60%):** primary demand drops in the segments that CAN recycle (cemented carbide + WHA), but the 28-44 kt/yr permanent primary demand floor (chemicals + steel/superalloy losses) is **recycling-immune**
- TGN's US$415/MTU OPEX competitive with recycling, but realized price is supported by structural floor in chemicals + DFARS Tier 2/3 mandate
- P(wtd) drops to A$2.50-3.00 (11-14×) **only if** the failure is severe (60%+ recycling); realistically blended ceiling is 35-40%, so the P(wtd) impact is contained to A$4.00-4.30 (18-19×)

**Industry-specific recycling rate breakdown (v10.1 AUDIT-2):**

| Industry | 2025 Demand (t/yr) | % Global | Recycling Rate | Marginal Increase Possible? |
|----------|-------------------:|---------:|----------------:|----------------------------:|
| **Cemented carbide (cutting tools)** | ~38,000 | 27% | **55%** | NO — already near ceiling (Sandvik 90%, Plansee 91%) |
| **Cemented carbide (mining/wear/other)** | ~45,000 | 32% | **40%** | Marginal — closed-loop growing but dispersed scrap |
| **Steels / superalloys** | ~20,000 | 14% | **15%** | NO — pyrometallurgical losses are structural |
| **Mill products / WHA** | ~16,000 | 11% | **22%** | YES — WHA is 90-95% W, economic to recycle |
| **Chemicals (WF6/SCR/AMT/WS2)** | ~12,000-22,000 | 11% | **5%** | NO — chemistry-bound, 7-15× harder than metal recycling |
| **Other (filaments, pigments, electronics)** | ~3,000-4,000 | 3% | **5%** | NO — dispersed end-uses |
| **BLENDED** | **~140,000** | **100%** | **~30%** | — Realistic ceiling 35-40%, NOT 60% |

**Probability:** 10-20% (cemented carbide + WHA segment growth; 0% for chemicals + steel segment)

**Early warning signal:** Sandvik/Kennametal announce 50%+ recycled content (would be 50%→55% in cutting tools, marginal impact on overall blended rate); secondary APT price drops **AND** chemistry-bound segment recycling also rises (currently impossible without major technology breakthrough)

**Position action:** Trim 10-20% if recycling mandates become binding AND the chemicals segment also sees meaningful recycling growth (currently impossible)

**Why this could happen (and why it mostly WON'T eat TGN's primary demand):**
- EU CRMA is binding regulation **but only applies where chemistry permits recycling** — WF6, SCR, WS2, pigments are exempt by physics
- Defense primes also have recycling mandates **but for the cemented carbide segment** (already at ceiling)
- Closed-loop economics are attractive at US$3,000/MTU APT **for cutting tools**, not for chemistry-bound segments
- **The "recycling will eat primary demand" narrative is overstated** — practical blended ceiling 35-40%, NOT 60%. The 28-44 kt/yr permanent primary demand floor is **recycling-immune** by chemistry and pyrometallurgy
- **TGN's value capture is in the chemistry + DFARS Tier 2/3 segments**, not the cutting-tool cemented carbide segment — so even severe recycling growth in cutting tools doesn't materially affect TGN's realized price

## Appendix: Permanent Primary Demand Floor (v10.1 REFRAMING per AUDIT-2, July 18, 2026)

The "Permanent Primary Demand Floor" is the volume of tungsten demand in **industries where recycling CANNOT close the gap**, even with closed-loop scaling. This floor is the structural reason APT prices cannot collapse to long-term marginal cost — and is the most defensible driver of the TGN thesis.

|----------|---------------------:|---------:|----------------:|-------------------------:|-------------------|
| **Cemented carbide (cutting tools)** | ~38,000 | 27% | **55%** | ~21,000 | Sandvik 90% buyback target, Plansee 91% mature closed-loop |
| **Cemented carbide (mining/wear/other)** | ~45,000 | 32% | **40%** | ~18,000 | Closed-loop growing but dispersed scrap; some downcycling |
