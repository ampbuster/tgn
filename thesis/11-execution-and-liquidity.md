## The "Sell the News" Trap

### The Problem

In small-cap mining stocks, when a major catalyst drops (e.g., FID, offtake, drill assays), the stock often **gaps up sharply in pre-market or open**. Retail investors see the PR and place **market buy orders** to "get in."

**The Trap:** Large players ("whales") use this retail FOMO as exit liquidity. They:

1. Watch for the catalyst announcement
2. Place market sell orders into the morning gap-up
3. Retail market buy orders fill their sells
4. The stock dumps 15-25% from the morning high by close
5. Retail investors are now underwater on day one
6. Whales have used retail as exit liquidity

### The Solution

**Never market-buy into a catalyst morning PR spike.** Instead:

- Place **limit buy orders at or below** the previous day's close BEFORE the catalyst
- If missed, wait for the morning volatility to settle (often 1-2 hours)
- Look for a higher low to establish support
- Then scale in with limit orders

**The retail flow is predictable. Don't be the exit liquidity.**

## Limit Order Scaling Strategy

### Pre-Set Limit Orders (Before Catalyst)

**Step 1:** Before any major catalyst (FID, offtake, drill assays), identify key price levels:

- **Support level:** Where you'll start buying
- **Resistance level:** Where you'll start trimming
- **Target prices:** Where you'll trim larger positions

**Step 2:** Place limit orders at these levels BEFORE the catalyst:

| Order Type | Price | Quantity | Purpose |
|------------|-------|----------|---------|
| Limit Buy | A$0.18 | 25% of target | First add on weakness |
| Limit Buy | A$0.20 | 25% of target | Second add on weakness |
| Limit Buy | A$0.22 | 25% of target | Third add at base |
| Limit Sell | A$0.35 | 15% of position | First trim, recapture capital |
| Limit Sell | A$0.42 | 15% of position | Second trim |
| Limit Sell | A$0.50 | 15% of position | Third trim |

**Step 3:** Force the FOMO algorithms to pay your premium:
- Your limit sells are above the current price
- Algorithms chasing momentum must bid up to your level
- You capture the volatility premium

### Why This Works

- **Pre-commitment** removes emotional decision-making
- **Limit orders** ensure you don't chase momentum
- **Scaling** smooths your entry/exit over time
- **FOMO premium** is captured by disciplined investors

## The "House Money" Trim

### The Psychology

After a major catalyst causes a sharp gap-up, the position is suddenly in significant profit. The temptation is to hold everything and "let it ride." But this is psychologically dangerous:

- A 30% drawdown from the high feels catastrophic, even if still in profit
- A macro shock (BOJ unwind, recession) can hit any time
- A 5-7 year hold requires surviving multiple drawdowns

### The Solution: Trim 15-20% at First Major Gap-Up

**The "House Money" Strategy:**

1. **First major catalyst triggers gap-up** (e.g., FID + offtake)
2. **Stock gaps from A$0.22 to A$0.40** (typical 80% gap-up)
3. **Trim 15-20% of position at the high**
4. **Recover your INITIAL CAPITAL** (the "house money")
5. **The remaining 80% is "pure house money"** — you can hold through anything

**Example:**
- Initial position: A$100,000 cost basis
- Trim 20% at A$0.40 = A$0.40 × 20% = A$0.08 per original share
- Cash recovered: ~A$80,000 (recovers most of initial A$100,000)
- Remaining 80% is "house money" — psychologically stress-free

**Benefits:**
- **Eliminates psychological stress** — you can hold through drawdowns
- **Reduces position size** — easier to manage in illiquid market
- **Locks in some gains** — even if thesis fails, you've recovered capital
- **"Moonbag" remains** — you keep the bulk of upside

### When to NOT Trim

- If conviction is at maximum and you believe in 100x+ upside
- If the catalyst is incomplete (e.g., FID but no offtake yet)
- If technical setup suggests much higher prices (e.g., breakout pattern)
- If you've already recovered capital through prior trims

## Defending Against Macro Shocks

### The Risk

Even with a perfect thesis, macro events can cause sharp drawdowns:
- BOJ rate hike (yen carry unwind)
- Global recession
- Trade war escalation
- Debt ceiling impasse
- Black swan events

### The Protocol

**1. Maintain a Cash Reserve**

- Don't go 100% into TGN
- Hold 10-20% of portfolio in cash/stable assets
- This cash can be deployed on drawdowns

**2. Pre-Set Buy Limits Below Current Price**

- Set limit buys at A$0.18, A$0.20, A$0.22
- These will trigger on sharp drawdowns
- You'll add to position at lower prices

**3. Don't Check Price Daily**

- Daily checking causes emotional trading
- Check weekly or only on catalyst days
- Focus on the thesis, not the price

**4. Have a Stop-Loss (Catastrophic Insurance)**

- A$0.12 stop-loss
- Protects against total loss (bankruptcy, administration)
- Don't tighten it further (would trigger on normal volatility)

## Liquidity Management

### The Hard Truth

TGN daily volume: 200-500k shares
Your position (100k shares illustrative): 20-50% of daily volume

**You cannot exit quickly.** Trying to sell 100k+ shares in a day will crash the price.

### The Solution: Gradual Scaling

**On the way UP (trimming):**
- Sell 5-10% of position per week
- Use limit sells above current price
- Let the market come to you

**On the way DOWN (adding):**
- Buy 5-10% of planned add per week
- Use limit buys below current price
- Don't catch a falling knife

**During Catalysts (volatile):**
- Pre-set limit orders BEFORE the catalyst
- Don't make decisions during the chaos
- Wait for the dust to settle (1-2 weeks post-catalyst)

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### 1. Over-Trading

- Setting and resetting limit orders
- Reacting to every price movement
- **Solution:** Pre-commit, then leave alone

### 2. Emotional Decision-Making

- Selling on fear during drawdowns
- Buying on greed during spikes
- **Solution:** Use the protocols, not emotions

### 3. Position Sizing Mistakes

- Going too large (concentration risk)
- Going too small (insufficient upside)
- **Solution:** Stick to 6-9% portfolio allocation (4-AI audit consensus)

### 4. Stop-Loss Hunting

- Setting stop-loss too tight (triggers on normal volatility)
- Setting stop-loss too loose (doesn't protect)
- **Solution:** A$0.12 is the analytical answer

### 5. "Diamond Hands" Trap

- Refusing to take profits even when extreme
- Holding through major re-ratings
- **Solution:** "House money" trim at major catalysts

### 6. Tax Mistakes

- Not considering capital gains
- Short-term vs long-term treatment
- Wash sale rules
- **Solution:** Tax-aware trimming strategy
